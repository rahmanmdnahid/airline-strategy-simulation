## 📋 Project Overview

This repository documents a comprehensive **8-quarter airline industry simulation** completed as a capstone project in Strategic Management. The simulation placed ForD2 Airlines in a **12-airline competitive environment**, requiring real-time strategic decisions across every functional area of business operations.

Each quarter presented new scenarios, competitive pressures, and data signals that demanded cross-functional decision-making — integrating financial analysis, marketing strategy, operations management, human resource management, and corporate social responsibility into a unified competitive strategy.

**Situation:** The airline entered with a full fleet deployed but only filled 49.7% of seats (dead last). Despite generating Rank #1 gross revenue at $3.93M, operating expenses of $3.96M created a $144K loss.

**Key Problem:** Scale-demand mismatch. Too many seat miles available, not enough passengers.

**My Decisions (Marketing/Financing/Services):**
- 100% marketing focus on Region 3 — avoid expansion costs, build dominant position
- Joined online consortiums — increase reach at minimal cost
- $1,000 environmental causes investment — brand image building
- Zero sales force — keep fixed costs low in early stage
- No financing changes — sufficient cash and credit available

## 🎯 Strategic Framework — The 5 Pillars of ForD2

### 1. Operational Consistency
Four routes maintained unchanged for 8 quarters. Fleet, maintenance, and fuel strategy held constant. This predictability enabled accurate financial forecasting and allowed the team to attribute performance changes to external factors or specific decisions rather than baseline operational noise.

### 2. Conservative Financing
Zero debt throughout the entire simulation. No stock issuance, no dividends paid. Fully self-funded operations preserved financial flexibility, eliminated interest cost drag, and maintained maximum optionality for every strategic quarter. Cumulative cash strength enabled confident decision-making without financial pressure.

### 3. Human Capital Investment
+2% above-market wages combined with $8,000/quarter training budget delivered measurable operational returns: quality score improved from 70 → 86 (Q4 peak) and reliability maintained a 93–95% band — consistently in the upper tier of the 12-airline field. This validates stakeholder theory: employee investment creates competitive operational advantage.

### 4. Data-Driven Demand Management
Load factor monitored against break-even every single quarter. Marketing budgets dynamically adjusted — increased when load factor fell below the 56.9% threshold, reduced when demand was healthy and costs needed trimming. This variable-cost approach to marketing unlocked Q3's peak profitability by eliminating marketing overhead when it wasn't needed.

### 5. ESG Leadership
Environmental causes as CSR area. Above-market wages as stakeholder commitment. Q8 diversity hiring decision aligned with D&I values. The simulation demonstrated that business performance and ethical responsibility are complementary — ForD2 maintained $735K cumulative profit while being a social leader in the field.

## 🔄 What We'd Do Differently

1. **Q1 Fleet Right-Sizing** — Deploy fewer aircraft initially, match fleet to demonstrated demand rather than projections. The $144K Q1 loss was avoidable.

2. **Market Research Investment** — Purchase industry intelligence reports in Q1–Q2 for competitive visibility and better demand forecasting.

3. **Earlier Route Expansion** — Once stable at Q5 profitability ($697K cumulative), explore a 5th route to grow revenue base beyond Rank #12.

4. **Cargo Evaluation in Q3–Q4** — Begin cargo service earlier when cash was strongest and break-even window was feasible.

5. **Sales Force Trial** — Test 1–2 salespersons in Q3 to assess revenue uplift against fixed cost addition.

6. **Ancillary Revenue Earlier** — Introduce food fees and seat selection optimization in Q1 rather than Q5.
